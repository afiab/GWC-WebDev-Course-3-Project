Notable Females

Katherine Johnson

Katherine Johnson was an American mathematician. She played a crucial role to the success of the first and subsequent U.S. crewed space missions with her calculations of orbital mechanics.

Mary W Jackson

Mary Jackson was an American mathematician and aerospace engineer. She was NASA's first black female engineer.

Dorothy Vaughan

Dorothy Jean Johnson Vaughan was an American mathematician and human computer. Vaughan was both a respected mathematician and NASA's first African-American manager.

Margaret Hamilton

Margaret Elaine Hamilton is an American computer scientist, systems engineer, and business owner. She was director of the Software Engineering Division of the MIT Instrumentation Laboratory, which developed on-board flight software for NASA's Apollo program

Chieko Asakawa

Chieko Asakawa is a blind Japanese computer scientist. She developed the IBM Home Page Reader, which is the most widely used web-to-speech system available.
